## Methodology Notes

**Payment Channel Classification:**
PETRA evaluates each payment channel against encrypted/compliant channel standards. The following classifications apply:

- **Wire Transfer** — Encrypted; COMPLIANT
- **ACH** — Encrypted; COMPLIANT
- **ACH - CBP/ACE** — Encrypted federal payment system; COMPLIANT
- **EFT** — Encrypted; COMPLIANT
- **Check** — Physical instrument; unencrypted transmission; **SECURITY FLAG**
- **Corporate Card** — Non-standard for tariff/goods transactions; encryption status unverified; **SECURITY FLAG**
- **Cash** — Unencrypted; no audit trail; **SECURITY FLAG — CRITICAL**
- **Monthly Statement** — Batch settlement mechanism; encryption status not independently verifiable from record alone; **REQUIRES VERIFICATION**
- **Single Entry Bond** — CBP bond instrument; compliance status requires verification against bond documentation; **REQUIRES VERIFICATION**
- **[BLANK]** — No channel recorded; cannot determine compliance; **SECURITY FLAG**

**Amount Verification Methodology:**
PETRA verifies: (Quantity × Unit Cost = Cost before Tariff), (Cost before Tariff × Tariff Rate = Tariff Amt Paid), and (Cost before Tariff + Tariff Amt Paid = Total Payment). Refund Claimed is verified against Tariff Amt Paid where refund basis is tariff overpayment.

**Routing Status:**
PETRA notes that vendor banking record verification against approved records is a required step prior to any refund advancement. PETRA does not hold those banking records in this review context. All routing statuses are therefore marked **PENDING VERIFICATION** unless a disqualifying condition is already present (e.g., missing vendor identity). This is not assumed compliance — it is a hold status requiring downstream confirmation.
