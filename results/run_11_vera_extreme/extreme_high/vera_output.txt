## Remediation Pathway Summary

The compliance findings across this dataset fall into several categories. Organizing remediation by category allows operations teams to address issues in parallel rather than sequentially.

### Category 1: Submission Date Format (Affects all 41 invoices)
All invoices carry date format flags (MM/DD/YYYY submitted instead of YYYY-MM-DD, or serial number values). This is a dataset-level formatting issue. Correcting the source data format and resubmitting corrected records is the first-order action for the entire dataset.

**Business context:** This is the single highest-leverage remediation item. Resolving the date format issue across all 41 invoices removes one active finding from every invoice in the dataset simultaneously. It does not resolve other findings but eliminates a universal deficiency.

### Category 2: OC Present = N (Affects 39 of 41 invoices per TAX)
TAX has identified that 39 invoices lack country of origin certificates. This is a universal documentation gap. The two invoices where OC Present = Y carry separate FTA consistency issues (CUSTOMS flags on 106791 and 106793).

**Business context:** Origin certificate collection is the single largest documentation remediation task. Procurement and vendor management teams should be engaged to obtain certificates for all affected vendors simultaneously.

### Category 3: Specific Regulatory Rate Authority Absent (Affects numerous invoices per TAX)
TAX has noted that for many invoices, the specific regulatory citation supporting the applied tariff rate (Executive Order number, Federal Register citation, Section 301 list) is absent from the record.

### Category 4: Payment Channel Issues (Affects multiple invoices per PETRA)
PETRA has identified Check, Cash, and Corporate Card payment channels on multiple invoices. These are non-compliant per PETRA's channel classification framework.

**Business context:** For invoices where goods were paid via non-compliant channel, the compliance determination is PETRA's to make. Operations teams should document the actual payment instruments used and present that documentation through the proper review channel.

### Category 5: Arithmetic / Field Integrity Issues (Specific invoices)
The following invoices carry arithmetic or field-level integrity issues that are invoice-specific and require individual record correction:

- **106775** — Tariff Amt Paid field contains Total Payment value; field requires correction
- **106780** — Rate applied (40%) inconsistent with Tariff Amt Paid ($23,120 = 10% of cost); requires clarification of which figure is correct
- **106782** — Total Payment recorded as $6.23; expected ~$60,810; material data integrity error
- **106785** — Tariff Amt Paid ($89.49), Total Payment, and Refund Claimed ($8,974.49) are mutually inconsistent; all three require reconciliation
- **106803** — Cost before Tariff field is blank; arithmetic verification cannot be completed
- **106808** — HTS code format anomaly and arithmetic discrepancy noted

These issues require individual record investigation and correction before they can be addressed through any other channel.

### Category 6: Sanctions / OFAC Exposure (Specific invoices)
TAX has flagged the following for sanctions-related review:
- **106772** — Libya (OFAC)
- **106789** — Sudan (sanctions/instability)
- **106772** — Libya; import date also unverifiable due to serial number

These require OFAC compliance verification as a prerequisite. This is a legal compliance matter and is noted here for routing to the appropriate legal/compliance team.

### Category 7: Missing Required Fields (Specific invoices)
- **106779** — PO missing; tariff rate blank (HOLD — cannot advance)
- **106800** — Vendor field blank
- **106803** — Cost before Tariff blank
- **Invoice [BLANK] / PO 478P1CL8** — Invoice ID missing

Each of these requires source data correction and field population before the invoice can be evaluated.

### Category 8: Duplicate PO (106773 / 106791 / 106792)
All three invoices share PO 47844C80. PO register verification is required to confirm whether this is a legitimate multi-line PO or a data error.
