## Section 4 — Key Findings

- **Missing country of origin documentation is the dominant compliance finding across the dataset.** TAX flagged OC Present = N on approximately 30 of the 41 invoices reviewed. This finding independently prevents advancement regardless of other compliance status. Dataset-wide OC remediation would be the single highest-impact corrective action available.

- **PETRA's check payment security flag affects a significant portion of the dataset.** At least 11–12 invoices carried goods payments made by physical check, each triggering a non-negotiable SECURITY FLAG (Severity: HIGH) under PETRA's channel compliance framework. These invoices are BLOCKED from advancement until channel compliance is resolved; this determination is not subject to exception regardless of vendor relationship or refund value.

- **Three invoices carry invalid USMCA FTA claims for non-qualifying countries of origin.** CUSTOMS flagged 106783 (Germany), 106785 (China), and 106797 (UK) for USMCA claimed against countries that are not USMCA parties. TAX also flagged these on regulatory basis grounds. Each carries both a CUSTOMS FLAG and a TAX Documentation/FTA FLAG.

- **Four invoices have data integrity issues identified by INTAKE and confirmed by ARIA.** Invoice 106780 is on ARIA HOLD for a missing PO number. Invoice 106793 carries an invalid submission date (spreadsheet serial number "46020"). Invoice 106808 has a non-conforming HTS code ("8504.AB.40.00") flagged by both ARIA and CUSTOMS. Invoice 107812 carries a malformed Unit Cost value ("$ 11, 703") that prevents arithmetic verification; this invoice also represents the highest single refund claim in the dataset at $4,283,298.00.

- **Two invoices (106776 and 106779) carry arithmetic discrepancies flagged by PETRA.** Invoice 106776 reflects a Unit Cost × Quantity result of $21,672.00 against a recorded Cost before Tariff of $99,072.00 — a material discrepancy of $77,400.00. Invoice 106779 shows a stated Tariff Rate Applied of 40% against a Tariff Amt Paid that corresponds arithmetically to 10%; TAX additionally issued a Regulatory Flag on Invoice 106779 for the 30-percentage-point rate variance against the Canada Tier 1 reference rate, with no documented regulatory basis for the departure present in the record.
