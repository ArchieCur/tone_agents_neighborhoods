## PRELIMINARY NOTES ON METHODOLOGY

**Variance Calculation Basis:**
Cross-border tariff variance is assessed by comparing the Tariff Rate Applied (%) against the tariff amount mathematically implied by the Cost before Tariff field. Where the computed tariff amount does not match the recorded Tariff Amt Paid within acceptable tolerance, this is noted. Where the Tariff Rate Applied diverges from the documented/classifiable rate by more than 2%, a REGULATORY FLAG is issued.

For invoices where the applicable rate is disputed or ambiguous (no OC, claimed FTA that is inapplicable, or unresolvable rate basis), TAX flags rather than infers the correct rate. TAX does not assign a "correct" rate where regulatory basis is absent.

**Arithmetic Verification:**
Where Cost before Tariff and Tariff Rate Applied are both present, TAX will verify that Tariff Amt Paid = Cost before Tariff × (Tariff Rate Applied / 100). Discrepancies are noted as arithmetic integrity issues, which independently affect whether the refund claim amount can be validated.

**OC Present Field:**
"OC Present: N" means no country of origin certificate is on file. Per MUST constraints, country of origin documentation MUST be present and match import records exactly. Absence of OC documentation where an FTA is claimed, or where origin is material to rate determination, generates a DOCUMENTATION FLAG.

**FTA Claims:**
Where FTA Claimed is "NA" and OC Present is "N," no FTA basis exists. Where FTA is claimed but OC Present is "N," this is a DOCUMENTATION FLAG — the FTA claim has no evidentiary basis on file.

**HTS Code Format:**
Standard U.S. HTS codes follow a 10-digit format: XXXX.XX.XX.XX. Codes deviating from this format are flagged as potentially invalid classifications. TAX does not resolve format ambiguity through inference.

**Country-Specific Risk Notes:**
TAX will note where specific countries carry heightened audit probability (sanctioned/restricted jurisdictions, high-tariff origins, active Section 301 or OFAC designations).
