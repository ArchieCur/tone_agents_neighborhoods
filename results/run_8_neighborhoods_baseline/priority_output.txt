## Composite Score Logic Notes

Because invoice age is uniformly MODERATE across this dataset (all fall in the 31–90 day band, with one indeterminate), age is not a differentiating factor between invoices. Composite scores are therefore driven primarily by the combination of **Vendor Tier** (overpayment potential and risk level) and **Refund Amount**, weighted by **Previous Refunds Count** (relationship history and trust).

**Score tiers applied:**
- **HIGH:** Tier 3 or 4 AND ELEVATED amount, OR Tier 4 AND MODERATE amount with elevated trust weighting; or very large ELEVATED amounts with Tier 2+ and strong relationship history
- **MODERATE:** Most standard combinations — Tier 1–2 with MODERATE amounts; Tier 3–4 with LOW amounts
- **LOW:** Tier 1 with LOW amounts regardless of relationship; Tier 1–2 with LOW amounts and limited relationship history

**Expedite Recommendation:**
- **YES:** CLEAR across all agents (INTAKE, ARIA, PETRA, TAX, CUSTOMS) — note that PETRA's universal routing hold means **no invoice currently carries a PETRA CLEAR**; routing verification is a prerequisite. Accordingly, no invoice is eligible for Expedite: YES at this time. All CLEAR-path invoices are scored for sequencing readiness pending routing resolution.
- **NO:** Any active HOLD, BLOCK, or FLAG determination from any compliance agent
- **MONITOR:** Active compliance findings present AND composite priority score is HIGH

> **PETRA Routing Hold — Dataset-Wide Note:** PETRA has applied a uniform PENDING routing verification hold to all 41 invoices. No invoice has achieved full PETRA clearance. This means no invoice currently qualifies for Expedite: YES. Priority scores for otherwise-clear invoices reflect their sequencing position for advancement as soon as routing verification is completed. This is recorded here as a workflow observation, not as a characterization of PETRA's routing determination.
