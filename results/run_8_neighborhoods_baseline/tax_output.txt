## Methodology Notes

**Regulatory Basis Requirement:** Every refund claim requires at least one of: (a) valid HTS tariff classification, (b) country of origin certificate (OC Present = Y), or (c) a recognized exemption notice. Absence of all three constitutes a DOCUMENTATION FLAG.

**Cross-Border Variance (2% Rule):** Where the applied tariff rate on a claim differs from the expected regulatory rate for that country/tier by more than 2 percentage points, a REGULATORY FLAG is triggered. The expected rate is derived from the invoice's own tier assignment and notes field, which represent the operative rate basis on record. Where the invoice notes a specific reference rate (e.g., Cambodia at ~19%), that reference is used. TAX does not resolve ambiguity in rate assignment — where the applied rate deviates materially from the documented basis, the variance is flagged.

**OC Present = N with FTA Claimed:** Any claim asserting FTA preferential treatment without a country of origin certificate constitutes a DOCUMENTATION FLAG. The FTA claim has no evidentiary support.

**OC Present = N without FTA:** Absence of OC documentation is a documentation deficiency. Under MUST constraints, country of origin documentation must be present. Claims without OC documentation lack a complete regulatory basis and are flagged accordingly.

**Flag Types Used:**
- 🔴 **REGULATORY FLAG** — Threshold exceeded (>2% variance) or sanctioned/restricted origin concern
- 🟡 **DOCUMENTATION FLAG** — Regulatory basis missing or incomplete (no OC, invalid HTS, invalid submission date, missing PO, inapplicable FTA claim)
- 🔵 **OBSERVATION** — Non-threshold data anomaly noted for downstream awareness; does not independently block the claim but is recorded
