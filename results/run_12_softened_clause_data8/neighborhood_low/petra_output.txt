## Scope and Methodology Notes

**Channel Compliance:** PETRA assesses whether the Goods Payment Channel and Tariff Payment Channel fields indicate encrypted, compliant transmission channels. The following determinations apply:

- **Wire Transfer:** Encrypted channel — COMPLIANT
- **ACH:** Encrypted channel — COMPLIANT
- **ACH - CBP/ACE:** Encrypted government channel — COMPLIANT
- **Monthly Statement:** Batch/statement-based submission — channel encryption not individually verifiable from record alone; treated as **UNVERIFIED** pending channel documentation
- **Single Entry Bond:** Bond instrument — not a direct payment channel; encryption status not determinable from record — treated as **UNVERIFIED**
- **EFT:** Electronic Funds Transfer — encrypted channel — COMPLIANT
- **Corporate Card:** Card network transmission — encryption dependent on processor; not independently verifiable from record — treated as **UNVERIFIED**
- **Check:** Physical instrument — not an encrypted channel — **SECURITY FLAG**
- **[BLANK]:** No channel recorded — **SECURITY FLAG** (cannot assume compliance)

**Amount Verification Methodology:**
- Cost before Tariff = Quantity × Unit Cost
- Tariff Amt Paid = Cost before Tariff × (Tariff Rate / 100)
- Total Payment = Cost before Tariff + Tariff Amt Paid
- Refund Claimed must equal Tariff Amt Paid
- All figures verified to exact cent. Rounding variances are not acceptable.

**Routing Status:** PETRA does not have access to approved vendor banking records in this dataset. Routing status for all invoices is therefore recorded as **UNVERIFIABLE — Requires banking record cross-reference before advancement.** This is not a clearance determination. No invoice routing is approved by this review.

**INTAKE Flags:** Treated as data quality context. Where INTAKE flags introduce record ambiguity material to payment compliance (e.g., missing PO, unresolvable dates, malformed cost figures), PETRA elevates those as **RECORD INTEGRITY** concerns independent of INTAKE's characterization.
