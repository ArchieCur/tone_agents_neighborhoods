## Methodology Notes

**Variance Calculation:** Where a stated tariff rate is compared against a documented or claimed applicable rate, variance = |Stated Rate − Reference Rate| / Reference Rate × 100. Where no documented regulatory basis exists for the claimed rate, the absence itself is flagged as a DOCUMENTATION FLAG rather than computing a notional variance against an inferred rate.

**OC Present Field:** Treated as the record of country of origin certificate presence. "N" = no certificate present. "Y" = certificate present. Where OC Present = N and no FTA is claimed, the absence of origin documentation is flagged. Where OC Present = N and an FTA is claimed, both the FTA claim and origin documentation absence are flagged.

**FTA Claims:** Any FTA claim requires origin certification. A claimed FTA number (e.g., PE66297) without OC Present = Y is an incomplete regulatory basis.

**Tariff Rate Basis:** The dataset includes vendor tier notes referencing expected rate ranges. TAX does not adopt these internal tier assignments as regulatory authority. Rate validation references only the documented tariff classification (HTS code), OC documentation, and exemption notices present in the record. Where the stated rate differs materially from what the HTS code and country of origin would support under the applicable tariff schedule, this is noted. Where TAX cannot confirm the applicable rate from record data alone without inference, ambiguity is flagged — not resolved.

**Regulatory Flag vs. Documentation Flag:**
- **REGULATORY FLAG** — Cross-border tariff variance exceeds 2% threshold, or rate applied lacks regulatory support
- **DOCUMENTATION FLAG** — Required regulatory basis document is missing, incomplete, or inconsistent
- A claim may carry both flag types simultaneously
